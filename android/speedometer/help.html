Frequently Asked Questions:

GPS speed differs from that indicated by the vehicle's speedometer.
Vehicle speedometers are typically designed to slightly overestimate speed as a safety measure and to comply with regulations that prohibit them from underreporting. This ensures drivers are not unintentionally speeding. In contrast, GPS devices calculate speed based on satellite data, offering a more precise and unbiased measurement. Variations can also result from factors like tire size, pressure, or wear affecting the vehicle's calibration.
The speed never shows up, but the location appears in other apps.
GPS Speedometer Premium relies solely on the GPS sensor to provide accurate speed readings. Other apps may show your location using Wi-Fi or mobile networks, which are helpful for faster location initialization and indoor positioning — especially when GPS signals are blocked by ceilings or buildings. However, this network-based data lacks the precision needed to calculate speed accurately. To ensure reliable results, speed is only displayed when a strong GPS signal is available. More information is available at https://www.gps.gov/systems/gps/performance/accuracy/

The speed appears delayed.
GPS updates your location roughly every one second. A current location alone is not sufficient for measuring the speed. However, by using the formula:
speed = distance / time
Distance: The GPS calculates the distance between the current location (newest position) and the previous location (from 1 second ago)
Time: The time interval between these two location updates is usually 1 second, but can occasionally take longer if GPS signal is lost, which is taken into account
it's possible to calculate it. This means the speed displayed is not the instantaneous speed at the exact moment but rather the average speed over the previous second. Additionally, the GPS signal must travel between satellites and the device, which increases the delay even further.

Also please note that enabling Smoother speed changes in the Advanced section adds further delay.
Is the app compatible with Android Auto?
GPS Speedometer Premium is not compatible with Android Auto. Currently, Google only allows specific categories of apps—such as navigation, media, and messaging—to be integrated with Android Auto. Speedometer apps do not fall within the supported categories and are therefore not eligible for Android Auto compatibility.

While it's technically possible to adapt the app for Android Auto, the strict rules and limitations would demand significant changes to its design and functionality. Ultimately, it would need to operate much like a full navigation app—displaying a fullscreen map with only a small speed indicator—which goes against the main purpose of the app.
